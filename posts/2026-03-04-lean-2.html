Prove that in a group homomorphism, 1 is mapped to 1

Signature of the proof is like 

theorem hom_maps_identity {G H : Type} [Group G] [Group H]
   (f : G →* H): f 1 = 1 := sorry
A few things are to be noted.
We want to prove some things about Groups G and H. We don't write: (G H: Group) since G is defined in Mathlib as a typeclass, not a type. This is because G is a general group. If we define it is a type then its members will have to be concrete values, like Nat is a type and 1, 2, 3 are values of that type.
Secondly, →* is notation for "bundled monoid homomorphism", specifically for MonoidHom: 

structure MonoidHom (G H : Type) [MulOneClass G] [MulOneClass H] where
  toFun : G → H
  map_one' : toFun 1 = 1
  map_mul' : ∀ a b, toFun (a * b) = toFun a * toFun b
If f is homomorphism, then f(1) = 1. Here are some ways to prove that 

theorem hom_maps_identity {G H : Type} [Group G] [Group H]
   (f : G →* H): f 1 = 1 := f.map_one

theorem hom_maps_identity1 {G H : Type} [Group G] [Group H] 
    (f : G →* H) : f 1 = 1 := by
  have h1 : f 1 * f 1 = f (1 * 1) := (f.map_mul 1 1).symm
  have h2 : f (1 * 1) = f 1 := by rw [one_mul]
  have h3 : f 1 * f 1 = f 1 := by rw [h1, h2]
  have h4 : f 1 * f 1 = f 1 * 1 := by rw [h3, mul_one]
  exact mul_left_cancel h4

theorem hom_maps_identity2 {G H : Type} [Group G] [Group H] 
    (f : G →* H) : f 1 = 1 := by
  have h1 : f 1 * f 1 = f 1 * 1 := by
    calc f 1 * f 1 
        = f (1 * 1) := by rw [f.map_mul]
      _ = f 1       := by rw [one_mul]
      _ = f 1 * 1   := by rw [mul_one]
  exact mul_left_cancel h1
